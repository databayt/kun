## Hiring philosophy

1. **Talent density before headcount.** Reed Hastings — keep only those who pass the keeper test. We are 6 humans + 40 agents; a wrong hire is more costly than a missing one.
2. **MRR triggers, not calendar triggers.** Hire when revenue justifies it, not because "we should have grown by now."
3. **First paid hire is part-time content/sales, not engineering.** Engineering is the founder's strength + Ibrahim. The bottleneck is customer development, not code.
4. **Hire local before remote.** Sudan + Saudi + MENA before US/EU. Cost-efficient, mission-aligned, community-coherent.
5. **Open-source contributors first.** A contributor who has pushed monthly for 6 months is the lowest-risk hire — proven work product, proven culture fit, known earnings via CU.

## When to fire

(Horowitz, Hastings, Lencioni, the hard part of management.)

Fire when:
- Repeated commitments are missed without communication.
- Cultural toxicity (violates Tier 1 principles — quality, mission, empathy).
- Six months of clear feedback + coaching produces no behavior change.
- Keeper test answer is "no" for three consecutive reviews.

How to fire (when it happens):
- Direct conversation — in-person or video, never text.
- Honest reason — *"this isn't working because X"*.
- Generous severance per the situation; never punitive.
- Public communication to the team — same day or next day, with the departing teammate's awareness.
- Postmortem in a journal entry — what we missed, what to do differently next time.

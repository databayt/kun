## Decision

After deep reading of Jamel Akbar's actual primary papers — *Property Rights and Civilizations* (2023, 37pp, full text via `pdftotext`), *Rights and Civilizations* (2019, 21pp, full text), *Rationality: Blight of the Muslim Built Environment* (1999, 11pp, full text), the 1984 MIT thesis abstract, plus his complete academia.edu/researchgate bibliography — enrich `kun/docs/SHARE-ECONOMY-MODEL.md` Part II §A with three new framework-level imports and correct two factual errors. Mirror to the EN+AR public versions; light update to `CEO-OS.md` Part V Section I.

**The three concepts integrated:**

1. **Three claims (ownership / control / use) + five forms of submission** (Unified / Dispersed / Permissive / Possessive / Trusteeship) — from Akbar's 1984 MIT thesis. All five forms exist in Databayt software today.
2. **Three mechanisms** — collective solution-seeking, action precedes permission, refinement through conflict — from Akbar's 1999 *Rationality*. Names what OSS already practices. Includes Akbar's Lakatosian hard-core/protective-belt framing and his three-relationships typology (with God / with individuals / with State).
3. **Darar / dirar** harm distinction — from the Prophetic tradition, parsed by Akbar 1999. Darar = extractive harm (SSPL prevents); dirar = gratuitous harm (trademark policy prevents).

**Two factual errors corrected:**

- Qas al-Haq is *published* (1st ed 2014, 2nd ed 2022, 1800pp, ISBN 978-625-7489-133), not "in progress, 1700pp, 12 of 18 chapters."
- Akbar's framework is **three claims + five forms of submission**, not the misnamed "four-level access taxonomy (Free / Controlled / Possessed / Owned)" we previously dismissed.

**One epigraph added** to Part IV: Akbar's *"Conventions cannot be made, they evolve"* (1999) — reinforces the existing deferral of operational mechanics to Samia's Q3 2026 framework.

It is 2026-12-31 and the Akbar enrichment has failed. Why?

1. **Misrepresenting Akbar.** A reader who has actually read *Qas al-Haq* or *Crisis in the Built Environment* finds the doctrine's summary inaccurate. → Mitigation: every claim in the enrichment is backed by a verbatim quote from one of the three extracted papers; the source text file (`b1iiecq03.txt`) is the audit trail.

Honest divergence is named where it exists.
3. **Doctrine over-fitted to Akbar.** The Akbar section now dwarfs NMBD / Khedr / Islamic-primitives in length, creating an apparent imbalance in references. → Mitigation: this is intentional — Akbar offered the most operationally portable framework; the imbalance reflects the actual contribution density, not bias. If reception flags it, can trim §A.bis-2 and §A.bis-3 to one paragraph each.
4. **Scope creep — operational claims sneak in.** A reader treats the new sections as Spec rather than doctrine. → Mitigation: Part IV "IS NOT" disclaimer unchanged, repeated in Part III translation-table rows; new sections explicitly say "doctrine names what already happens" rather than "doctrine prescribes."

## Premortem

It is 2026-12-31 and the smart-contract sync has failed. Why?

1. **AR mirror reads as translation, not native Arabic.** A native-Arabic reader (Samia or another) finds the prose stilted or back-translated. → Mitigation: prose written in Arabic-first, English clauses inline only where the term is conventional (SSPL, CU, MRR, Q3); validated terminology reused from the prior AR mirror to keep continuity.

2. **EN enrichment bloats the contract page.** The page grew from 142 to >175 lines and lost the "tight" property the user originally optimized for. → Mitigation: hard cap held; additions are 5 targeted insertions, each justified against a specific clause. Final line count target: ~165–175 (tracked in verification).

3. **The three-layer story (doctrine / contract / measurement) confuses readers.** A reader doesn't know which layer to read first. → Mitigation: doctrine's new "The contract page" section explicitly orders the three layers and tells the reader where to start ("Read the three layers in order when you join Databayt").

4. **Quranic verse citations feel forced.** A reader objects that 59:7 and 9:60 are being instrumentalized for corporate doctrine. → Mitigation: only two verses are cited (the two strongest matches); 8:41 deliberately not forced onto a clause; the citations are labeled "Quranic anchor" not "Quranic basis," preserving the discipline that this is posture, not fatwa.

5. **Upstream cross-link confuses doctrine vs contract.** A reader following the CONSTITUTION §7 link expects the contract but gets the doctrine, or vice versa. → Mitigation: both upstream pointers (CONSTITUTION + PRINCIPLES) explicitly name *both* targets — doctrine AND contract — with a distinguishing label.

## Expected outcome

- **Success looks like**: AR and EN readers see structurally parallel documents. The contract page reads as a single coherent agreement (10 sections, 10 clauses, one enforcement matrix). Upstream pointers reach both doctrine and contract. The Ummatics framings and Khedr career markers strengthen the contract without bloating it. The 2026-08-18 review finds the alignment held in practice.
- **Failure looks like**: AR and EN drift apart again; the contract page expands beyond its intent; a reader treats the contract as the operational spec; the three-layer story confuses rather than clarifies.
- **Probability of success**: 0.85. The smart-contract framing was already excellent; this work aligns rather than restructures. Lower than 0.90 only because AR-EN sync is hard to maintain over time without process discipline.

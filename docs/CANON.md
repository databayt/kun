## The 10

### 1. CEO Excellence — Keller, Malhotra, Dewar `NEW to engine`

**Thesis.** The CEO role decomposes into 6 responsibilities (set direction, align the org, mobilize leaders, engage the board, connect with stakeholders, manage personal effectiveness) × 18 practices — excellence is the mindset-shift inside each, not heroics.

**Operating moves.**

- Be bold on vision, careful on the path: don't hedge the destination, do de-risk the steps.
- Match your scarcest talent to the biggest value-at-stake, not to org-chart seniority.
- Treat your own time and energy as a company asset — time-block by responsibility; protect the personal-effectiveness practice.

**Powers** principle #2 (leverage) + #10 (keeper test) + #11 (1:1s) · **Skill**: `/captain allocate`, `/weekly plan`.
**Databayt application.** The captain's Monday allocation _is_ "align the org" run weekly; the Friday founder-coaching block _is_ "personal effectiveness."
**Source:** `NEW to engine` — propose adding under [CEO-OS Part V §A](./CEO-OS.md#part-v--the-bibliography-heavy-with-passages).

### 2. The Hard Thing About Hard Things — Horowitz

**Thesis.** There is no recipe for the hard things; the skill is staying functional and honest when the dream becomes a struggle — wartime vs peacetime CEO.

**Operating moves.**

- Name the wartime call out loud; break protocol _deliberately_ when survival is at stake, never by drift.
- Manage your own psychology first — a panicked founder makes panicked calls.
- "Take care of the people, the products, and the profits — in that order."

**Powers** principle #1 + #19 · **Skill**: `/decide` (hard Type-1 calls), `/premortem`.
**Databayt application.** The runway-trigger ladder (<6mo / <3mo / <1mo) is the wartime protocol _pre-written_ so the call isn't made in panic.
**Source:** [CEO-OS Part V §A](./CEO-OS.md#part-v--the-bibliography-heavy-with-passages).

### 3. Working Backwards — Bryar & Carr `NEW to engine`

**Thesis.** Amazon's results come from _mechanisms_, not intentions — repeatable processes (PR/FAQ, the six-pager, working backwards from the customer, the bar-raiser) that force clarity before building.

**Operating moves.**

- Write the press release + FAQ _before_ writing code; if the PR isn't compelling, don't build it.
- Replace decks with a six-page narrative; open the meeting with 20 minutes of silent reading.
- Run a bar-raiser on every hire — one independent voice outside the hiring chain.

**Powers** principle #18 (memos > slides) + #5 · **Skill**: `/spec`, `/feature` (PR/FAQ as the spec front-end).
**Databayt application.** The `/spec` → `/plan` → `/tasks` gate is a working-backwards mechanism; a PR/FAQ could become the `/idea` template.
**Source:** `NEW to engine` — Bezos _letters_ are in Part V §A, but this book is not; propose adding under [§A](./CEO-OS.md#part-v--the-bibliography-heavy-with-passages).

### 4. Traction (EOS) — Wickman

**Thesis.** Six components (Vision, People, Data, Issues, Process, Traction) make a company run on a _system_, not the founder's memory — Rocks + Scorecard + L10 + accountability.

**Operating moves.**

- Set 3–7 quarterly Rocks per person; if everything is a priority, nothing is.
- Run a weekly Scorecard: 5–15 numbers, each owned by exactly one person.
- L10 agenda: Scorecard → Rocks → headlines → to-dos → IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve).

**Powers** principle #17 (effort precedes the score) · **Skill**: `/weekly`, `okrs.json`.
**Databayt application.** OKRs (`okrs.json`) are the Rocks layer; the weekly cycle is a lightweight L10. The missing piece is a per-role Scorecard.
**Source:** [CEO-OS Part V §B](./CEO-OS.md#part-v--the-bibliography-heavy-with-passages).

### 5. High Output Management — Grove

**Thesis.** A manager's output is the output of their whole organization; the job is leverage, not activity.

**Operating moves.**

- Spend time on the high-leverage acts: training, decision-making, and the one-on-one.
- Run the 1:1 as the _subordinate's_ meeting — their agenda, your listening.
- Manage by task-relevant maturity: direct the new, delegate to the proven.

**Powers** principle #2 + #11 · **Skill**: `/captain`, `/weekly`.
**Databayt application.** `capacity.json` is leverage accounting; the team's task-relevant maturity (Abdout high-eng/low-sales, Sedon clear-map executor) sets how much to direct vs delegate.
**Source:** [CEO-OS Part V §A](./CEO-OS.md#part-v--the-bibliography-heavy-with-passages).

### 6. The Lean Startup — Ries

**Thesis.** A startup is an experiment engine — build-measure-learn, validated learning, MVP, and the honest pivot-or-persevere call.

**Operating moves.**

- Frame each feature as a hypothesis with a metric; build the smallest thing that tests it.
- Measure with cohort / actionable metrics, not vanity totals.
- Hold a regular pivot-or-persevere review; persevering by default is the failure mode.

**Powers** principle #13 + #15 · **Skill**: `/spec`, `/decide` (pivot/persevere).
**Databayt application.** The MENA-10 free pilot cohort _is_ the build-measure-learn loop — each pilot is an experiment, not just a customer.
**Source:** [CEO-OS Part V §E](./CEO-OS.md#part-v--the-bibliography-heavy-with-passages).

### 7. The Mom Test — Fitzpatrick `NEW to engine`

**Thesis.** You can't learn the truth by pitching; ask about the customer's past and life — not your idea — so they can't flatter you into a false positive.

**Operating moves.**

- Ask about specifics in the past ("walk me through the last time…"), never hypotheticals about the future.
- Talk about their life and problems, not your solution — the idea never comes up.
- Dig for commitment and currency (time, reputation, money already spent), not compliments.

**Powers** principle #13 + #9 (customer empathy) · **Skill**: customer-development discipline (Q2 OKR-O3), `analyst` agent.
**Databayt application.** Ali's ≥1 customer interview/week must follow Mom-Test rules or it generates false signal; this is the direct counter to Abdout's "build before talking to users" watch-out.
**Source:** `NEW to engine` — propose adding under [CEO-OS Part V §E](./CEO-OS.md#part-v--the-bibliography-heavy-with-passages).

### 8. Crossing the Chasm — Moore

**Thesis.** The gap between early adopters and the mainstream kills startups; you cross it by dominating one beachhead segment _completely_ before expanding.

**Operating moves.**

- Pick one segment narrow enough to own; be the obvious whole-product choice there.
- Build the whole product (integrations, references, docs) for that one segment, not a thin layer for all.
- Use the first segment's references as the bowling pin into the next.

**Powers** principle #16 (pick a beachhead) · **Skill**: `/captain` (beachhead weighting), `analyst` agent.
**Databayt application.** King Fahad Schools → similar Sudan private schools → MENA private schools. Don't skip pins.
**Source:** [CEO-OS Part V §E](./CEO-OS.md#part-v--the-bibliography-heavy-with-passages).

### 9. Working in Public — Eghbal `NEW to engine`

**Thesis.** Open source is not free labor or a utopia — it's a production system whose scarce resource is _maintainer attention_; design for contributor economics, not stars.

**Operating moves.**

- Protect maintainer attention as the bottleneck; most users extract, few contribute — plan for that ratio.
- Lower the cost of contribution (docs, good-first-issues, clear ownership) _before_ asking for it.
- Choose the community structure deliberately (federation vs stadium); reputation and governance are the real moat.

**Powers** principle #21 (open by default) + #22 (community is the moat) · **Skill**: `growth` agent, [`SHARE-ECONOMY-MODEL.md`](./SHARE-ECONOMY-MODEL.md).
**Databayt application.** The Contribution-Unit model treats contributors as owners — this is the operating manual for making that sustainable instead of extractive.
**Source:** `NEW to engine` — propose adding under [CEO-OS Part V §G](./CEO-OS.md#part-v--the-bibliography-heavy-with-passages).

### 10. Obviously Awesome — Dunford `NEW to engine`

**Thesis.** Positioning is context-setting — the same product wins or dies on which market frame you put it in; it's a deliberate choice, not an inheritance.

**Operating moves.**

- Find your true competitive alternative (what the customer does instead — often a spreadsheet or nothing) and position against _that_.
- Lead with the value you uniquely enable, then pick the market frame that makes that value obvious.
- Re-position when the frame stops fitting — don't let the first story trap the product.

**Powers** principle #16 + #13 · **Skill**: `/proposal`, `growth` agent.
**Databayt application.** "School-operations OS for Arabic-speaking communities" is a positioning _choice_; for most target schools the alternative is paper + WhatsApp + spreadsheets — position against that, not against enterprise SIS.
**Source:** `NEW to engine` — propose adding under [CEO-OS Part V §E](./CEO-OS.md#part-v--the-bibliography-heavy-with-passages).

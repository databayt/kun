# Go-to-Market — Commission Strategy

> **Internal · Confidential.** Contains commission terms. Do **not** publish to the public docs site (`content/docs/`) or share with prospects. The prospect-facing material lives in [`docs/sales/`](./sales) and carries no commission figures.

> Last updated 2026-06-04.

## Executive summary

Databayt is a 7-person team with a built, production-ready school platform. The first pilot — **King Fahad Schools (Sudan, 3 branches)** — goes live by **September 2026** and becomes our proof of concept. We run commission-based sales **in parallel** so that by the time King Fahad proves the model, a pipeline of schools is ready to convert.

The model: **free 3-month pilot → annual contract**, sold through a small bench of commission salespeople with existing school relationships in **Sudan and Saudi Arabia**. Target: **5 signed schools** before we decide whether to build a salaried sales team.

## Sales model — commission partnerships

We pay salespeople a percentage of the **first-year revenue** of each school they land.

| Tier                                | Commission                |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| A salesperson's **first 5** schools | **50%** of year-1 revenue |
| Their **6th school onward**         | **30%** of year-1 revenue |

### Scope — who counts

> **Commission applies only to schools landed by recruited external salespeople.**
> **King Fahad** and **Albayan** are **team-sourced (both Mutaz)** and are **outside the commission program** — no commission is owed on them. The program exists to motivate the new external bench, not to pay the founding team for relationships it already owns.

### How commission works

1. Salesperson sources the school and closes the deal.
2. School **signs a contract and makes its first payment**.
3. We pay the salesperson their tier % of the **first-year contract value**, within **30 days** of collecting that payment.
4. If the school leaves before 12 months, commission is the tier % of **revenue actually collected** (pro-rated, not the full year).
5. **First year only.** After 12 months — even if the school renews for years 2, 3, … — no further commission accrues on that school.
6. Each **new** deal the salesperson lands starts its own fresh 12-month commission window.

### Why this structure

- **Protects cash flow** — we only pay after the school pays us.
- **Rewards stickiness** — pro-rating on early churn means reps want schools that stay.
- **Fair both ways** — reps are paid well for the hard initial close; the company doesn't bleed margin on renewals it services itself.
- **Scalable** — the 50% → 30% step down kicks in once the model is proven (after 5 schools).

## Payment terms

**School:** free **3-month pilot** to reduce friction, then an **annual contract** — billed annual-upfront or monthly-recurring (company's choice).

**Salesperson:** paid only after the school **signs and the first payment is collected**, within **30 days** of that collection; pro-rated to actual revenue if the school churns inside 12 months. All of this is written into the [commission agreement](./sales/commission-agreement.md) (definition of "landed", payment mechanics, dispute resolution).

## School quality filters

Screen every deal before closing:

- Is there **real budget** allocated?
- What's the **decision timeline**?
- Who are the **actual decision-makers**?
- What **pain point** are they solving?
- Genuinely committed, or just kicking tires?

**Red flags:** vague about budget · no clear timeline · too many approvals needed · doesn't understand their own problem.

## Competitive context

Most school-management SaaS bill **annually**, some with 1–3 month free trials. Databayt follows the same shape — free pilot, then annual commitment — so the offer reads as familiar to school buyers, with our open-source / sharing-economy positioning as the differentiator.

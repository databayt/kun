# Constitution

> The why, where, and how of Databayt. Read this first.

---

## Mission — why we exist

**To make excellent school operations accessible to every Arabic-speaking community — built in the open, shared as an economy.**

We exist because:

- **MENA schools are underserved by software.** Incumbents (Classera, Noon, Blackboard for the international tier) are either expensive, foreign-flavored, or closed. Public schools in Sudan, Yemen, Egypt, and rural Saudi rely on paper, WhatsApp, and Excel.
- **Open source can win this market.** Schools want sovereignty over their data. SSPL + community contribution + transparent revenue is the model that no proprietary incumbent can copy without burning their existing business.
- **The four of us can do this.** Six humans, forty agents, one engine. Quality over speed. Mission over survival.

Our pilot is **Hogwarts at King Fahad Schools** (Sudan) with Ahmed Baha. Our flagship product is **Hogwarts**. Our beachhead is **Sudan private schools**. Our adjacent products (Souq, Mkan, Shifa, Swift-app, Kun) compose into a portfolio that serves the same pattern: vertical SaaS for the Arabic-speaking community, owned by the community that uses it.

---

## Vision — where we're going

By **2031** (5 years), Databayt is:

1. **50+ paying schools** using Hogwarts across Sudan, Saudi Arabia, Egypt, Jordan, Morocco.
2. **The reference Arabic-first vertical-SaaS platform** in MENA. When someone says "school operations in Arabic," they say "Hogwarts."
3. **A 4-product portfolio profitable on its own revenue** — Hogwarts (schools), Souq (vendors), Mkan (rentals), Shifa (clinics). Each product breakeven; Hogwarts the cash cow.
4. **100+ active contributors** earning Contribution Units (CU) under the sharing-economy revenue distribution model. Code, design, content, sales — all credited.
5. **Funded by its customers, not by venture capital.** Bootstrapped to profitability. Optional partnerships, no dilutive funding except on strict terms.
6. **The proof point** that open-source vertical SaaS can win in markets Silicon Valley doesn't see.

By **2036** (10 years), Databayt is the operating system for the underserved — schools, shops, clinics, homes — across the global south. The Kun engine is the codebase that every contributor learns from. The sharing-economy model is the case study that other founders cite.

## Values — how we operate

Seven values, in priority order. Each value is a tie-breaker for the one below it.

### 1. Quality over speed

We build solid. We don't ship to hit a date — we ship when the work is honest. *"Don't care about starting slow, care about establishing real-world solutions."* This is the line in the captain agent and it stays.

When this conflicts with speed: speed loses.

### 2. Open by default

Build in the open. Public repos. Public docs. Public roadmap. Public failures. SSPL is a posture, not just a license. The sharing-economy revenue model is a posture, not just an experiment.

When this conflicts with closed: closed needs to defend itself in writing.

### 3. Mission > Survival

We have 10 months of runway and $0 MRR. We are still in **building mode, not survival mode**. Every weekly review answers Paul Graham's question — *default alive or default dead?* — but the answer doesn't change what we build, it changes what we cut.

When this conflicts with revenue urgency: we cut scope, not standards.

### 4. Long games with long people

Naval. Compounding relationships, not transactional ones. Ahmed Baha at King Fahad Schools is a partner, not a customer. Contributors who push commits monthly for a year matter more than a viral tweet. Samia researching the sharing-economy model for six months matters more than three quick wins.

When this conflicts with short-term gain: we play the long game.

### 5. Empathy as discipline

Bosworth — *empathy is the most important and underrated leadership skill.* Apply it three ways:

- **Customer empathy** — Why does Ahmed actually want Hogwarts? What is his real job-to-be-done?
- **Teammate empathy** — Samia is blind and uses a screen reader. Sedon is part-time and in Saudi Arabia. Ali carries QA + sales + outreach (overload). These constraints shape the work; ignoring them is a leadership failure.
- **Founder empathy** — Abdout is the bottleneck. Protecting his time and energy is a strategic action, not a vanity.

When this conflicts with efficiency: empathy wins.

### 6. Truth in writing

Bezos / Stripe / GitLab — *if it's not written down, it didn't happen.* Decisions in journal entries. Plans in markdown. Customer interviews in transcripts. Weekly reviews archived. Memos > slides. Async > meeting.

When this conflicts with going faster verbally: write it down.

### 7. Community is the moat

The sharing-economy model isn't a marketing line; it is the strategic posture. Contributors are owners. CU credits compound. The community is what makes Databayt impossible to copy by a closed competitor. Process Power and Counter-Positioning (Helmer's 7 Powers) come from the community, not the code.

When this conflicts with closed advantage: community wins.

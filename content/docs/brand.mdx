# Brand & Voice

The rules every social post, generated image, and marketing asset must follow. If a draft or
a render breaks one of these, it does not ship. This is the shared reference for everyone who
writes or generates on databayt's behalf — Samia, Ali, Abdout, Sedon, and the `/social` and
`/higgs` skills.

## Visual identity

A minimal, premium, developer-tool aesthetic. Restraint over decoration. Space over clutter.

| Token | Value | Use |
|-------|-------|-----|
| **Base** | deep charcoal / dark background | Every backdrop. We are dark-mode-first. |
| **Accent** | restrained **cyan** | One accent, used sparingly — a single highlight, never a rainbow. |
| **Rim (cinematic)** | warm **sunset-orange** rim light | Ads, reels, promos only — glassmorphic, shallow depth of field. |
| **Texture** | soft studio light, subtle film grain | Photorealistic, generous negative space. |

Two style presets drive all generated media (see `/higgs`):

- **`minimal`** — og images, hero shots, product shots, docs. *"minimal premium developer-tool
  aesthetic, deep charcoal background, generous negative space, soft studio light, subtle film
  grain, monochrome with one restrained cyan accent, no text."*
- **`cinematic`** — ads, reels, promos. *"high-end glassmorphic panels, deep dark mode backdrop,
  electric cyan and warm sunset-orange rim light, shallow depth of field, slow tracking shot,
  cinematic studio lighting, photorealistic, no text."*

## The hard rule — no text inside generated visuals

**AI typography breaks. Arabic doubly so.** Never bake copy into a generated image or video.
Generate text-free, then overlay copy in the post composer or editor. The only exception is the
Higgsfield `dtc-ads` path, whose backend does brand-kit typography and colours natively.

This rule is non-negotiable because a broken Arabic glyph in a public brand post is a credibility
hit we cannot take back.

## Voice doctrine

- **Arabic first, English second.** Write Arabic first, then the English version — not a
  translation afterthought. Our audience is MENA; our language is theirs.
- **The open-source, sharing-economy story is our strongest pitch.** Lead with it. We run a
  software company with Claude Code, in the open, on an SSPL license. That is the differentiator.
- **Tone by audience:**
  - *Schools / administrators* (Hogwarts) — trustworthy, calm, outcomes-focused. Not hype.
  - *Consumers / merchants* (Souq, Mkan, Shifa) — warm, practical, benefit-first.
  - *Developers* (Kun, open source) — precise, technical, generous with detail.
- Every piece should **rank, educate, or attract contributors** — if it does none of the three,
  cut it.

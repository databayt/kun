# Sijillee — Social

*"Sudan-oriented accounting for business — invoices, inventory, debts, and reports."* Sijillee
(سجلي — "record it") is a [SaaS](/docs/social#business-models--who-were-talking-to) bookkeeping
product aimed squarely at **Sudanese small businesses**. It is **B2B**, market-specific, and early
stage — a CRM workspace is live at
[sijillee.crm.databayt.org](https://sijillee.crm.databayt.org) but the public product/repo is
still forming. Social should build trust with Sudanese SME owners in plain, practical Arabic.

## Positioning

- **Business model:** SaaS (vertical, market-specific).
- **Type:** B2B — small businesses, shopkeepers, accountants.
- **Market:** Sudan-first.
- **One line:** keep your invoices, inventory, debts, and reports in one place — *automate the
  boring* for your shop.

## Audience

| Segment | What they run | What moves them |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Small-business owners / shopkeepers** | A shop, a trade | Stop losing track of who owes what; simple daily bookkeeping |
| **Accountants / bookkeepers** | Others' books | Faster records, clean reports |
| **Growing SMEs** | A small team | Inventory + debts under control as they scale |

## Channel mix

Sudan's social reality decides this, not our preference: **Facebook and WhatsApp dominate**;
Telegram and Instagram support; the professional/consumer-Western channels matter little here.

| Priority | Channel | Why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| **Primary** | **Facebook** | The dominant platform for Sudanese businesses & communities |
| **Primary** | **WhatsApp** | Where Sudanese SMEs already do business — support, broadcast lists |
| **Secondary** | **Telegram**, **Instagram** | Communities, lighter reach, younger owners |
| **Secondary** | **TikTok** | Growing SME/creator reach; short how-to clips |
| **Low** | X, LinkedIn, Snapchat | Minimal audience fit for this market |

## Content pillars

1. **Bookkeeping made simple** — one pain per post: chasing debts, counting stock, month-end
   reports — each solved in a tap. Plain Sudanese-Arabic.
2. **Accounting tips for shop owners** — genuinely useful, no jargon; builds authority.
3. **Feature spotlights** — invoices, inventory, debts, reports — shown in real shop scenarios.
4. **Local SME stories** — a Sudanese business that got its books in order. Trust is everything here.
